About Me

As a researcher, my interests lie in NLP and data science. I'm currently focusing on leveraging Large Language Models (LLMs) to convert natural language into structured query languages, aiming to enhance question-and-answer systems in specialized knowledge domains.

In the realm of Neural Information Retrieval, I have been involved in creating semantic search engines. These engines efficiently analyze scientific papers by integrating exact match and semantic similarity approaches, thus improving information retrieval accuracy and efficiency.

Beyond these areas, my research extends to advancing lengthy text analysis, few-shot/zero-shot document categorization, and exploring other novel NLP tasks. I am also actively engaged in the research and development of retrieval-augmented language models.

My research endeavors aim to contribute to AI and computational science through innovative and practical solutions, encompassing ontologies, semantic web, and knowledge graph integration in AI systems.
